Stress

What you might already know: Stress is a feeling of emotional or physical tension. It can come from any event or thought that makes you feel frustrated, angry, or nervous. Stress is your body's reaction to a challenge or demand. What you should also be knowing:

  • Not all stress is bad.

  • Long-term stress can harm your health.

  • There are ways to manage stress

  • If you’re overwhelmed by stress, ask for help from a health professional.



    • Your affirmations in stressful times should look like :

  • - One day at a time , one step at a time

  • - Everything is going to be okay, I got this

  • - I’m going to focus on the things I love to get me through this



    • Anxiety

    What you might already know:

    Anxiety is a natural human reaction that involves mind and body. It serves an important basic survival function: Anxiety is an alarm system that is activated whenever a person perceives danger or threat. These sensations are part of the body's fight-flight response. They are caused by a rush of adrenaline and other chemicals that prepare the body to make a quick getaway from danger.

    What you also should know:

  • Anxiety Isn't "All In Your Head"

  • Anxiety Isn't Rational

  • Anxiety Isn't Unmanageable

  • Anxiety Isn't Something To Be Ashamed Of


    • Affirmations to help when you’re anxious:

  • I trust the process of life

  • All is well, I am going to be calm, happy and content

  • I possess the qualities I need to find solutions


    • Depression

    For a long time, no one really talked about depression at all. Now we’re working hard to change that — and we need to learn how to talk about depression in the right way. You can’t help someone with depression in any meaningful way without talking to them about it. That’s why it’s so important to have the courage to speak up and find your words.We have curated a list of symptoms that you might look into, but it is to be kept in mind that signs of depression can appear differently in individuals depending on their age, gender and other factors. So, it is nothing but unfair to compare yourself and your feelings with others that claim to be going through the same.. Common signs of depression:

  • Hostility, aggression, and persistent irritability

  • Changes in weight, eating patterns, and appetite

  • Lethargy or a significant decrease in energy

  • Reduced concentration

  • Defiant or rebellious behaviors

  • Changes in sleep patterns (excessive sleeping or difficulties sleeping)


    • What you can do about it:

    Depression can drain your energy, leaving you feeling empty and fatigued. This can make it difficult to muster the strength or desire to seek treatment. However, there are small steps you can take to help you feel more in control and improve your overall sense of well-being.

  • Meet yourself where you are Depression is common. It affects millions of people, including some in your life. You may not realize they face similar challenges, emotions, and obstacles.

  • Reward your efforts All goals are worthy of recognition, and all successes are worthy of celebration. When you achieve a goal, do your best to recognize it.

  • Do something you enjoy… …like listening to music Or spend time in nature Or spend time with loved ones Depression can push you to give into your fatigue. It may feel more powerful than happy emotions.

  • You may find it helpful to create a routine If depressive symptoms disrupt your daily routine, setting a gentle schedule may help you feel in control. But these plans don’t have to map out an entire day. Focus on times when you feel the most disorganized or scattered.


    • Consider clinical treatment You may also find it helpful to speak to a professional about what you’re going through. A general practitioner may be able to refer you to a therapist or other specialist.
